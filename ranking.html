Saving Private Ryan

Director: Stephen Spilberg

Release Date: July 24th, 1998

Staring: Vin Diesel, Tom Hanks, Tom Sizemore

Not just another great action movie, Saving Private Ryan is often regarded as the essential World War II movie. The movie manages to bring the viewer as close to the action as possible and make it feel so real without needing the viewer to have actually seen or experienced these monumentus events. The opening D-Day sequence is the pinnacle of this, showing not only bombastic action and strong moments of hereoism and leadership, but also showing the brutality of the war with numerous gruesume deaths and dismemberment. The movie keeps this tone all the way to the end and portrays our portagonists as soldiers trying to complete their mission and maintain as few casualties as possible rather than as larger than life heros. On top of this, all the scenes of the actual war are portrayed as a flashback of a veteran visting fallen comrades at a cemetary, once again showing the reality and cost of war. Overall, this movie is my favorite World War II flick due to its strict portrayol of reality and well directed, well acted, action sequences.

American Beauty

Director: Stephen Spilberg

Release Date: October 1st, 1999

Staring: Kevin Spacey, Thora Birch, Wes Bently

This one ended up being a pleasent suprise for me, saying as I had never heard of the movie until I watched it. In short, the movie is about several characters who fight the constructs that either themselves or society has put them in. The characters all go through a similar transition even though they vary significantly. From middle-age man trying to add more joy into his life, to a strict conservative veteran coping with homosexual feelings, to an angsty teenager trying to find her way in the world. The most notable character though is Ricky Fitts, a high school teenager who is looked down upon in the begining for not conforming to normal social constructs but in reality, Ricky is the only one who is truely happy and at peace with who he is which is what everyone else is seeking.

Back To The Future

Director: Roberth Zemeckis

Release Date: July 3rd, 1985

Staring: Michael J. Fox, Christopher Lloyd

Back to the Future manages to make a thrilling sci-fi movie that is also sleek and cool with a lot of good laughs throughout. Marty McFly, played by the charismatic stud Michael J. Fox. Match him with the crazy scientist who's life goal was acheiving time travel played by the eccentric Christopher Lloyd. The two play off each well mixed with a romp through time with goofs and gafs along the way and we have a timeless (lol) comedy.

Star Wars: Empire Strikes Back

Director: Irvin Kershner

Release Date: June 20th, 1980

Staring: Mark Hamill, Harrison Ford, Carrie Fisher

Star Wars: Empire Strikes Back is a matserpeice in multiple ways. For one, it does a great job of world building. Even with the tremendous amount of different planets and life-forms in the Universe of Star Wars, each one feels appropriate and believable within the world. Another stand out aspect of the movie are the characters. From the always lovable, yet sassy droids, to Han Solo and Chewbacca's smuggler duo just trying to get by, to Luke wanting to learn the ways of the Jedi while being conflicted by the fact that his friends lives are in danger. All these aspects come together to make the movie as a whole even better than the sum of its masterfully created and well though out parts.
